Vigo

Vigo is a city and municipality of Spain, part of the autonomous community of Galicia.

Located in the Northwest of the Iberian Peninsula, it straddles on the southern shore of an inlet of the Atlantic Ocean, the Ria de Vigo, the southernmost of the so-called Rías Baixas. Vigo is the most populated municipality of the province of Pontevedra and the whole region as well as also the biggest one in the country without the status of provincial capital. It has an area of 109.06 km2 (42.11 sq mi) and had a population of 292,817 in 2016. According to the Instituto Nacional de Estatística the city proper has 198,537 inhabitants, while the rest of the municipal population dwell in a number of rural and suburban parishes.

Vigo has a great variety of beaches along its coast. The city has 56 sandbanks, from the largest such as Samil and O Vao, to the most secluded in Saiáns and the paradisiacal beaches of the Cíes Islands. And these are some of my favorites.
